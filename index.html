HISTORY


In cryptography, the Caesar Cipher, also known as exchange cipher, Caesar code or Caesar exchange, is one of the most simple and well-known encryption techniques. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter of the text is replaced by another, which appears in the alphabet below it a fixed number of times. For example, with an exchange of three positions, A would be replaced by D, B would become E, and so on. The method is named after Julius Caesar, who used it to communicate with his generals.
